# Completion Rules

Use this reference after filling `references/knowledge-graph-template.md` with source content. Complete only the blank fields needed to make the material usable for undergraduate STEM self-study.

## Source Fidelity Rules

- Do not freely rewrite, embellish, or replace source text.
- Preserve original course wording unless it is inaccurate, unreadable, or structurally unusable.
- Prefer moving, grouping, or lightly formatting source text over generating new explanations.
- Keep source-derived content and supplemental content distinguishable.
- Do not fill a blank field merely because the template has that field. Fill it only when the absence blocks self-study quality.

## Supplement Hierarchy

When a blank field must be completed, use sources in this order:

1. Other parts of the same source file.
2. User-provided files or instructions.
3. Authoritative retrieved sources for time-sensitive or externally verifiable claims.
4. Stable disciplinary knowledge for non-time-sensitive STEM fundamentals.

Use searched content selectively. Add only the minimum needed to fill the blank and support the learning objective.

## What May Be Added

Add minimal supplemental content for:

- Missing definitions of symbols, units, variables, or notation.
- Missing prerequisite bridges required to understand the next step.
- Missing learning objectives when the section has clear source content but no explicit objective.
- Missing worked-example steps when the source provides the problem and final result but skips necessary reasoning.
- Missing exercise hints or short answers when the source already contains exercises intended for self-study.
- Missing caveats, assumptions, or version notes for time-sensitive claims.
- Missing answer and explainations for the given questions or quiz

## What Must Not Be Added

Do not add:

- New topics not implied by the source or user request.
- Motivational filler or broad background essays.
- Decorative examples that do not support a source concept.
- Unverified updates to versioned, current, or externally factual claims.
- Full solutions when a short hint is enough for self-study.
- Claims that contradict the source without noting the correction basis.

## Labeling Rules

For each completed blank field, record it in the knowledge graph's `Supplemental Content Log`.

Use one of these basis labels:

- `source-derived`: completed from another location in the source file.
- `user-provided`: completed from user instructions or attached materials.
- `verified-external`: completed from authoritative retrieved content.
- `stable-domain`: completed from stable STEM knowledge that is not time-sensitive.
- `model supplement`: extra contents generated by language model

## Minimal Rewrite Rules

When source wording is low quality but usable:

- Keep technical terms and notation unchanged.
- Split long source sentences only when readability requires it.
- Preserve formula order and variable names.
- Keep examples tied to the original numbers, diagrams, code, or data.
- Mark unclear source wording as uncertain instead of inventing a clean replacement.

## Completion Check

Before materialization, verify:

- Every supplement has a reason.
- Every supplement has a basis label.
- Time-sensitive supplements have authoritative sources or are left blank with a review note.
- The final learner-facing content still reflects the original course material rather than a newly authored lesson.
